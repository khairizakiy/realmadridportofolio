Real Madrid-Celta: all eyes on a Copa quarter-final place

Real Madrid host Celta this evening at the Santiago Bernabéu for a single-game knockout tie in the Copa del Rey Round of 16. This will be the first time the madridistas play on home turf this year, having been on the road for their opening four fixtures (9:30pm CET). Ancelotti's charges will be fully focused on securing their place in Monday's quarter-final draw.

Our boys haven't played in front of the home fans since 22 December, when they beat Sevilla 4-2 to close out 2024 on a high. With the fixtures still coming thick and fast this year, Real Madrid now face three successive games at the Bernabéu. After tonight's Copa clash, they'll welcome Las Palmas in LaLiga this weekend, and then Salzburg in the Champions League.

“This game will do us good, we want to see a reaction," underlined Ancelotti in the pre-match press conference. The Italian confirmed Lunin is set to start in the sticks against Celta, and has already named his squad as he looks to book a quarter-final spot. Mbappé and Vini Jr., the team's top scorers so far this season, are both on the list.

Celta This evening sees the Galician side face their fourth away trip in this edition of the Copa del Rey. They come into the Round of 16 following impressive wins over Racing Santander (2-3), Salamanca UDS (0-7) and San Pedro (1-5).

Defeat in Spanish Super Cup final

Defeat in the final of the Spanish Super Cup against Barcelona at the King Abdullah Sports City stadium in Jeddah (Saudi Arabia). The match started at a frenetic pace with chances galore. The first two were for Barcelona with a shot by Lamine Yamal and a header by Raphinha that forced Courtois into two good stops. Our team's response was as immediate as it was forceful. Mbappé received the ball with plenty of space in front of him and drove into the box, where he danced past Balde with a fine dribble and slotted past Szczesny.

Only five minutes were on the clock and Real Madrid had already taken the lead, but Lamine Yamal equalised midway through the first half after a solo run. Ancelotti‘s side came close to taking the lead again in the 24th minute. swung in the corner and Tchouameni's header forced Szczesny into a fine save. The game was a constant back and forth and on 35 minutes referee Gil Manzano blew for a penalty against Camavinga for a foul on Gavi at the behest of the VAR. Lewandowski converted.

It was further complicated by Raphinha converting with a deader from Koundé-s long pass to make it 1-3. And before half-time, Balde made it 1-4 with a counter-attack. Madrid could have made it 2-4 after the break when Rodrygo's volley hit the crossbar, but Raphinha made it 1-5. In the 56th minute, Szczesny was sent off following a VAR review for bringing down Mbappé on the edge of the box when he had already gone past him. Rodrygo took the free-kick and beat Iñaki Peña to make it 2-5 which was how the game ended.
